Terminal Improvements for Super Bowl XLVII

New Orleans International Airport (MSY), built in increments over the preceding 55 years was preparing for an influx of people for the Super Bowl in 2012. After years of heavy use, signs of wear had become all too apparent—the airport did little to reflect the city in which it was located.

Tasked with modernizing the interior of the terminal without the ability for reconfiguration, the design team took on the challenge of connecting a series of incongrous spaces of varying proportions and functions that comprise the terminal. The solution that emerged was to establish a flow between the differing spaces through a uniform set of design elements that are adapted for utilization in each existing space.

Scope of Work

The improvements were limited to the public spaces of the airport, shown in white on the plans. Renovation to the back of house spaces would occur in phases over the following two years, but these renovations were minimal due to the impending opening of the new airport terminal in 2019.
