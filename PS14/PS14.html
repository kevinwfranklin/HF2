Prototype for Hillside Dwelling

Unlike many cities where the poorest strata of the population is peripheralized to the surrounding mountainsides, in Los Angeles, the wealthiest strata lives above the often smoggy urban valley. In these communities, houses are built in the image of a typical suburban mansion. Extensive amounts of earthwork make for normative flat-slab dwellings.

Located on an especially steep hillside in between rows of mansions, this live-work project is an attempt to reimagine the way that these hillsides are inhabited. By orienting the design to the topography, an equal amount of excavation and backfilling simplifies construction.

An initial sitpulation for this project was that it had to be constructed from readily producible components. The aggregation of a relatively simple module allows for a systematic approach that gives the form an intrinsic flexibility. As such it is adaptable to any hillside location and programmatic variation.
